## 1. Upcoming Presentation Milestones

### A. Midterm Product Progress Presentation (Due Next Week)
* **Schedule:** To be delivered during next Tuesday's class period.
* **Duration:** 5 minutes.
* **Format:** Strictly 3 slides, broken down as follows:
  * **Slide 1: Purpose & Scope:** Clarify the overall purpose of the project so the entire class understands its boundaries and goals.
  * **Slide 2: Product Status & Mini Demo:** Provide a list of features completed or in-progress. Teams are encouraged to use a status light indicator format (Green = Done; Red = In Work; Yellow = Issues Encountered). Conclude with a brief live walkthrough or mini-demo of the current functional codebase.
  * **Slide 3: Future Roadmap:** Outline specific features planned for completion by the end of the semester.
* **Logistics:** The Product Owner is responsible for presenting or delegating the presentation to another team member.

### B. Final Course Presentation (End of Semester)
* **Duration:** 10 to 15 minutes.
* **Format:** Informational Commercial ("Infomercial") parallel to classic literary/cinematic Hero Story structures.
* **Required Presentation Elements:**
  1. **The Problem:** Establish a critical customer "pain point."
  2. **The Expert (Part I):** Introduce research/data validating that the problem exists.
  3. **Failed Solutions:** Demonstrate why pre-existing systems or alternative methods fail to resolve the pain point.
  4. **The Solution:** Formally introduce the main developed product.
  5. **The Expert (Part II):** Re-engage the expert/analytical data to verify the product effectively solves the problem.
  6. **Testimonies:** Show video/evidence of actual users testing and working with the software outside the developer's local machine.
  7. **Product Demo:** Provide a complete functional walkthrough of the operational features.
  8. **Freebies:** Detail the project "stretch goals" completed to add unexpected value for the customer.
  9. **Sense of Urgency:** Share critical "lessons learned" regarding teamwork, engineering blockades, and individual insights.
* **Participation:** Must feature audible voice contributions from every single participating team member.

## 2. Technical Project & Documentation Requirements

### A. Current Documentation Sprint: Installation Plan
* **Focus:** Weeks 8 and 9 are focused on drafting and completing the **Installation Plan**.
* **Target Audience:** The end-user (client, operator, or system administrator), *not* software developers. Technical setups for development environments belong in the Software Developer Plan.
* **Scope:** Must comprehensively explain how to install and configure the client environment, server infrastructure, and associated databases.
* **Previous Deliverables:** Complements the previously drafted Requirements (SRS), Design (SDD), and Test Plans.

### B. Feature Planning & Accountability
* **Individual Feature Velocity:** Each team member is expected to complete **5 to 7 discrete features** over the course of the semester (translating to a minimum of 25 features total for a standard 5-member team).
* **Comprehensive Engineering Ownership:** Feature planning documents must map out the entire full-stack lifecycle—Front-End, Back-End, and Database components—for that feature. Developers are responsible for engineering their feature completely, not just their siloed subsystem role.
* **Role Accountability:** Cross-functionally, engineers must use their explicit team role (e.g., Back-End Lead) to ensure that *all* features from *all* team members are correctly integrated, reviewed, tested, deployed, and monitored in the live system.
* **GitHub Tracking:** Every single feature, task, or documentation assignment must be tracked as an active GitHub Issue. Planning templates must accurately link to these unique issue numbers and trace back directly to official SRS, SDD, and Test references.
* **Infrastructure as a Feature:** Technical tasks such as constructing or debugging automated CI/CD workflows (e.g., GitHub Actions) qualify as valid, grade-eligible features.

### C. Grading Policy & Resubmissions
* **Competency-Based Mastery:** The course does not offer arbitrary extra credit. Instead, students are allowed to update and **resubmit their project work for additional points** based on instructor feedback. 
* **Review Checklist:** Students must explicitly execute and check off the peer/team review criteria boxes before submission to avoid immediate grading deductions.

## 3. Individual Classwork & Career Readiness

### A. BYU-I Gold Career Certificate Execution
* **Informational Interview Volume:** The "long pole" item for completing the Gold Certificate is the informational interview mandate. Students must perform **5 additional interviews** on top of the Silver (5) and Bronze (1) benchmarks, matching a total course volume of **11 informational interviews**.
* **Interview Methodology:** Recommended to follow a structured three-part framework derived from *The 20-Minute Networking Meeting* and *The 2-Hour Job Search*:
  1. **Core Q&A:** Inquire about their profession, day-to-day work, and organization.
  2. **Professional Advice:** Seek actionable recommendations on resume polishing, industry literature, or critical skills.
  3. **Network Expansion:** Always ask the interviewee for introductions to other industry professionals to continue building connection layers.
  4. **Follow-Up:** Issue a formal thank-you message within 24 hours of the engagement.
* **Networking Strategy:** Leverage immediate organic links (friends, family, university alumni, and local ecclesiastical directories) to bridge connections across degrees of separation.

### B. Training Modules & Digital Portfolio Construction
* **AWS & Cloud Services:** Students are currently processing AWS foundational cloud modules. Individuals who have previously completed this fundamental coursework for another curriculum can submit proof of completion to exempt themselves.
* **Ethics Sprint:** The final 3 weeks of the semester will shift exclusively to the ethics curriculum, which comprises two quizzes and a major core assignment focused on engineering risk management.
* **GitHub Portfolio Artifacts:** Portfolios must feature an articulate README detailing the product scope, concrete explanations of completed project stretch goals, and explicit documentation of technical lessons learned.
* **Professional Branding:** Students must publish a professional LinkedIn post detailing engineering lessons learned from their activities, complete with a traceable hyperlink routing directly to their GitHub code repository.
