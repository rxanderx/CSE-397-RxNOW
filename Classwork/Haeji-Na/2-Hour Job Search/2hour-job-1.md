# Haeji Na 3-Hours Job Search Part 1 Summary

**Part 1**

**What is a LAMP list?**  
A LAMP list is a target company list that helps organize a job search. LAMP stands for List, Alumni/Advocacy, Motivation, and Posting. It helps job seekers choose which companies to focus on first instead of applying randomly.

**What is the resume black hole?**  
The resume black hole is when someone applies online to many jobs but never hears back. Their resume may go into an online system and never be seen by a real person. This is why networking and having an advocate can be more helpful than only applying online.

---

## Chapter 1: List

**How much time should this take?**  
This step should take about 70 minutes.

**What should be in the Company List?**  
The company list should include companies I may want to work for. It should include company names, locations, possible advocates, motivation score, number of job postings, and whether I qualify.

**What company sizes have you primarily applied for?**  
I have mostly looked at medium and large companies, but I also included some smaller companies if they had cybersecurity, IT, or technology-related jobs.

**What 4 approaches should you use to create your list?**  
The four approaches are:
1. Dream employers
2. Alumni or people I know
3. Job postings
4. Similar companies or related industries

---

## Chapter 2: Advocacy

**How much time should this take?**  
This step should take about 20 minutes.

**Why should you have an advocate in the company?**  
An advocate is helpful because they can help me learn more about the company and may refer me or give advice. Having a person inside the company can help my application stand out more than only applying online.

---

## Chapter 3: Motivation

**How much time should this take?**  
This step should take about 20 minutes.

**What are 5 of your motivations?**  
My five motivations are:
1. Cybersecurity or technology-related work
2. Good location or remote work option
3. Good company reputation
4. Opportunities for learning and growth
5. Work that connects to my skills and long-term career goals

**What is your motivation scale?**  
My motivation scale is from 1 to 3.

- 1 = I am highly interested in this company
- 2 = I am somewhat interested
- 3 = I am less interested, but I would still consider it

---

## Chapter 4: Posting

**How much time should this take?**  
This step should take about 20 minutes.

**What is your threshold for the number of postings a company might have?**  
My threshold is based on how many job postings seem related to my career goals. If a company has more relevant postings, I give it a higher score.

**What is your scale for this column?**  
My posting scale is from 1 to 5.

- 1 = Very few job postings
- 2 = A few job postings
- 3 = Some job postings
- 4 = Many job postings
- 5 = A strong number of relevant job postings

---

## Summary

Prioritization can improve my job search because it helps me focus my time and energy on the best opportunities. Without a clear system, it is easy to apply to many jobs without a strong plan. That can make the job search feel stressful and random. A LAMP list helps me organize companies by interest, job openings, possible advocates, and how well I fit the role.

This method also helps me avoid spending too much time on companies that may not be a good match. For example, if a company has no advocate, low motivation, and few job postings, I may not want to focus on it first. On the other hand, if a company has a high motivation score, several job postings, and someone I can contact, it should become a higher priority.

The advocacy part is also important because online applications alone may not be enough. If I can talk to someone who works at the company or knows about the company, I may learn useful information and improve my chances. Overall, prioritization helps me be more organized, more confident, and more intentional in my job search. Instead of applying everywhere, I can focus on companies that match my skills, goals, and interests.
