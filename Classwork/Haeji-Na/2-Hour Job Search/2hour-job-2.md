## Chapter 6: Advocacy

**How long should this task take?**  
This task should take about 20 minutes.

**What is the 6-point email?**  
The 6-point email is a short message used to ask someone for advice or insight. It should be simple and respectful of the person’s time. The main goal is not to ask for a job, but to ask for a short conversation.

The six points are:

1. Keep it fewer than 75 words.
2. Ask for insight and advice, not job leads.
3. State the connection first.
4. Ask the request as a question.
5. Explain my interest clearly.
6. Make the message more about the contact than about myself.

**What are three troubleshooting solutions?**  

1. If the person does not respond, I can follow up after a few business days.
2. If I still do not get a response, I can try another contact at the same company.
3. If my emails are not working, I can make the email shorter, clearer, and more focused on asking for advice.

## Chapter 7: Track

**How long should this task take?**  
This task should take about 10 minutes.

**What is the 3B7 Routine?**  
The 3B7 Routine is a follow-up system. After I send an outreach email, I follow up after 3 business days if I do not hear back. If there is still no response, I follow up again after 7 business days. This helps me stay organized and not forget who I contacted.

### Outline the 3B7 Diagram on page 148

The 3B7 routine can be outlined like this:

1. Send the first outreach email.
2. Wait 3 business days.
3. If there is no reply, send a short follow-up email.
4. Wait 7 business days.
5. If there is still no reply, send one more follow-up or move on to another contact.
6. Track all contacts, dates, and replies in a spreadsheet or list.
7. Focus more on people who respond and seem willing to help.
