# Joseph Tolley 2-Hour Job Search Part 1 Summary

**Part 1**
* What is a LAMP list? 

It is a 70 minute process to find potential employers. It is important to seek what is actually desired work for yourself. The list itself will consist of at least 40 potential employers with advocates, postings, and motivations considered.

* What is the resume black hole?
  
Essentially when there is little to no hope of success. That may mean submitting an online application that won't likely be read. Or it might be sending out a million so-so applications to so-so fits. Employers can get swamped with resume's and unless you have a way to make your resume standout, it will remain in that 'black hole'.
  
**Chapter 1: List**
* How much time should this take?

  40 minutes
  
* What should be in the Company List? 

A list of 40 potential companies that interest you, have alumni or connections, are trending in the field, or are simply hiring. This can be quite varied and exceeding 40 is highly recommended.

* What company sizes have you primarily applied for?

  Stage 4- Over 500. I see now that this is not a great approach.
  
* What 4 approaches should you use to create your list?

Dream employers are like your first-choice employers, perhaps longshots. Alumni employers involves utilizing connections and links you might have. Posting search involves checking job postings to find employers actively hiring. Trend-following which is an approach to find industry specific areas and perhaps find less obvious employers.
  
**Chapter 2: Advocacy**
* How much time should this take?

  Ten minutes
  
* Why should you have an advocate in the company?

Sometimes being qualified and a great fit for a company is not enough. With connections inside the company of choice you have a much higher chance of being considered, assuming the advocate can help your chances. It is sometimes a matter of who you know rather than simply being a matter of qualifications.

**Chapter 3: Motivation**
* How much time should this take?

  5 Minutes
  
* What are 5 of your motivations?

First is Family/Professional balance, I do not want a job where I cannot get enough family time. Secondly, I would like a job where relocation is not necessary, whether that means local or remote. Third, I want to be confident in my position, I want to feel adequate for the position. Fourth would be salary and wages as I want to be able to comfortably provide for my family. A fifth would be liking what I do, an interesting workload is far superior than a workload of monotony. 

* What is your motivation scale?

With a ranking of 1-5, 5 is the top tier and 2 is the least. First choices are given 5's, second choices are 4's, and so on until the 2's which are low choices that even if they work out, it probably isn't worth pursuing anyway. A rating of 1 is reserved for those employers one knows very little about and a rating cannot be assigned without more information.

**Chapter 4: Posting**
* How much time should this take?

  15 Minutes
  
* What is your threshold for the number of posting a company might have?

  My threshhold is any postings, I will be happy to find multiple positions, but if there is one posting that is a posting I am going to be interested in.

* What is your scale for this column?

I intend to use a 3 point scale that would start at a 3 for a posting with a position I am looking for. A two would be if other positions are available from the employer. A 1 would be those that have no postings or available positions

**Summary**
* A short reflection (250-300 words) on how prioritization can improve your job search.

Prioritization is essential to creating an efficient process for job searching. By ranking potential employers I can focus on the more likely to succeed posts and forgo those that are not likely or positions I wouldn't like anyway. Having a large list of companies will allow me to have many options and would help me to feel more confident in my chances of employment. 

The advocate ranking is the least important to me in all honesty, I'm not the best at maintaining superficial connections so unless I have a genuine relationship with the person, I am not likely to consider this category. Motivation factor is huge, if its a position I don't want to do then unless there truly is no other option, I shouldn't pursue it. If I were, it would only be in the short term and I would much rather find a good long-term fit for employment. I agree with the sentiment that you need to love what you do in order to be happy in the position.  Postings number is something that I find very useful, an employer actively seeking employees is the most likely to respond to my seeking efforts. I want to hear back from my applications and unless they are looking, its not likely. A higher score in posting is an indicator that I need to be urgent in applying for best success.

In all, I am first looking for jobs I am qualified to do, jobs and careers I want to do, and from employers actively seeking. Having an advocate would be the cherry on top. After I have exhausted these top tier candidates I can look further down the list for postings that are good enough for now. I see no harm in courting many employers, the more entries in the lottery the higher the success!
