It's what I get for what I put effort in. It is certainly hard to quantify and I think finding the right target is worth all means of effort.

First-degree connections, second-degree connections, third-degree connections, group connections, and no connection.

**Chapter 7: Track**
*How long should this task take?

10 Minutes

*What is the 3B7 Routine?

It is a method to track your own outreach, three business days to see if there is a response. If there is, we probably have a booster. If not, we may have an obligate. At seven days it would be a good idea to send a follow-up email to the contact.  It is just fine to hedge our bets, too. Making contact with another person isn't going to harm you any. 

**Summary**

There is a lot of power behind tracking your shots versus a fire and forget type of approach. Choosing, tracking and contacting better choices are like sending a warhead rather than a cannonball. Maybe that cannonball was all that was needed, and that would be one lucky shot indeed! Another way to see it is like a cybersecurity pentest where that booster or super-booster is the payload we are looking for. 
I struggle with the whole concept simply because I struggle with connection entirely. When I do try to make contact it sounds superficial and going through the motions. This 5 point email idea is an excellent 
idea that I will be utilizing most certainly. I like the idea of keeping it short in particular, nobody wants a long email about how great someone else is. It also shows that you value their time enough to not take too much of it.

While I am not a big advocate for contacts I do see the benefits. Prioritizing the best options does limit the amount of anxiety associated with trying to make a professional relationship to a stranger, online. 
For me, there's frst degree and then there's cold-calling. I've never been one to get a word out through someone and have any sort of success. Either I'm talking to the person with the power to help me out, or there's shots in the dark. I do believe other people may be more natural with connection and can better utilize the differing tiers and degrees of contact. Personally, the best preperation I can do is knowing what the company does, wanting to be a part, and being able to do it.
