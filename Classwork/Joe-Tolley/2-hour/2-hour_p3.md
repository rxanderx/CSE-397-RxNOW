**Chapter 9: Discuss**
What is the Ben Franklin effect?

- His method was to ask a favour, in order to win favour. It's quite a smart method to gaining the confidence of a person and I'm surprised by how effective it seemed to be. He said that somebody who has done you one favour, is more likely to give you another. It is simple and brilliant.

What is TIARA?

- Trends, insights, advice, resources, and assignments. These are questions that a candidate job seeker can ask in order to break the ice and get to know the employer better. These questions allow the candidate to show that they care about the employment and its mission. They help to frame the interviewer as more of a mentor and therefore more likely to recommend the job seeker for moving forward. These are designed to give the seeker a leg-up on the competition by breaking awkwardness, showing interest, using the employer as a mentor, and raising the likelihood of success.

**Chapter 10: Follow-up**
* Outline the Harvest Cycle

- This is a systemic way to maintain contact, keep your name up, and increase likelihood of success. After an information interview, you should send a thank you email the next day. Afterwards, a monthly email is recommended to keep your name in their memory and show your interest in the opening. These emails should have another thank you, a recap of the advice, how you have applied the advice, and what more they can teach you to help your chances. This further boxes that booster into a position of being a mentor to you and more likely to recommend your resume over the competition. Yopu

A short reflection (250-300 words) on how prioritization can improve your job search.

Prioritization is essential to creating an efficient process for job searching. By ranking potential employers I can focus on the more likely to succeed posts and forgo those that are not likely or positions I wouldn't like anyway. Having a large list of companies will allow me to have many options and would help me to feel more confident in my chances of employment.

The advocate ranking is the least important to me in all honesty, I'm not the best at maintaining superficial connections so unless I have a genuine relationship with the person, I am not likely to consider this category. Motivation factor is huge, if its a position I don't want to do then unless there truly is no other option, I shouldn't pursue it. If I were, it would only be in the short term and I would much rather find a good long-term fit for employment. I agree with the sentiment that you need to love what you do in order to be happy in the position. Postings number is something that I find very useful, an employer actively seeking employees is the most likely to respond to my seeking efforts. I want to hear back from my applications and unless they are looking, its not likely. A higher score in posting is an indicator that I need to be urgent in applying for best success.

In all, I am first looking for jobs I am qualified to do, jobs and careers I want to do, and from employers actively seeking. Having an advocate would be the cherry on top. After I have exhausted these top tier candidates I can look further down the list for postings that are good enough for now. I see no harm in courting many employers, the more entries in the lottery the higher the success!

**Application**
* Outline a Informational Interview (Using Chapter 8 and 9 activities, page 241)

An information interview is a way to get your name into the mind of the employer as well as deciding on whether it is a good fit for you anyway. It begins with a simple request from a recruiter or booster to learn more about the company. This involves things like what they do, what you like about the company, what more information you would like to know, etc. It helps the interview be less awkward and having good small talk, rapport, and interest will increase the likelihood that the employer might choose your application. Getting the booster to think of themselves as a mentor to you is a huge boost that every candidate should try to take advantage of. Someone who does a favour for you will likely do another and the interview is in itself a favour to you. Asking good questions will show them you are serious about their company and what they do. It also gives you a chance to show how you could bring talent and effort to support them in their mission. The final piece is to maintain contact after the interview. Keeping your name up and continuing to leverage their advice is a great way to give your resume a better chance at being accepted for a desired position.
