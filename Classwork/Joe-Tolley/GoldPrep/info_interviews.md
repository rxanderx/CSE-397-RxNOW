1. Epic Systems, I learned about Epic and what they do. Talking to a recruiter I found that they do a LOT of medical software and services. They design systems that are used everyday in the medical field by
   millions of Americans. They have very good pay, and are willing to train and take new graduates. A downside I noticed is it is not an option to not relocate for the position I was inquiring about. They
   have a heavy workload as well, which I would expect from such a high paying salary. If I were younger it would be more tempting, and I'm sure I will apply anyway, but with a family this company may not be a
   best fit and I would be willing to settle for less pay for a better fit with my lifestyle.

2. CED logistics is a company that specializes in connecting the distributor to the customer. The recruiter I spoke to had a great powerpoint that laid out some of the basics about what they do, which is diverse.
   The most interesting part for me was they provide support for technical parts and builds, including robotics. They have locations all over the states including Idaho and the surrounding Northwest which was a
   plus for me and relocation was likely but not entirely necessary, depending on what I'd do. They have a training regiment that takes 1 to 2 years and frankly that was a plus for me too since I am in a state
   of perpetual nervousness about being thrown into the fire without a clue what I'm supposed to do. Overall, I thought they would be a good potential fit and I will be giving them another look most definitely.
