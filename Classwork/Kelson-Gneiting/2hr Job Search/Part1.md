**Part 1**
* What is a LAMP list?

LAMP is a structured way to prioritize employers instead of applying randomly. You build a target list (L) and score each company by advocacy (A), motivation (M), and postings (P) so your effort goes to the strongest opportunities first.

* What is the resume black hole?

The resume black hole is when applications are submitted through crowded online portals with little visibility, no referral, and no follow-up path, so your resume is unlikely to be seen by a decision maker.

**Chapter 1: List**
* How much time should this take?

About 40 minutes.

* What should be in the Company List?

At least 40 target companies that are realistic for my skills and interests, each with a link, an advocate rating, motivation score, posting score, and qualify rating.

* What company sizes have you primarily applied for?

Mostly medium-to-large organizations, including enterprise tech and government/defense contractors.

* What 4 approaches should you use to create your list?

Dream employers, alumni/network employers, currently posting employers, and trend-based employers in growing areas.

**Chapter 2: Advocacy**
* How much time should this take?

About 10 minutes.

* Why should you have an advocate in the company?

An advocate improves resume visibility, helps bypass the black hole, and can increase the chance of getting a screening interview.

**Chapter 3: Motivation**
* How much time should this take?

About 5 minutes.

* What are 5 of your motivations?

1. Work on machine learning and AI systems.
2. Build strong technical growth toward an ML engineer path.
3. Find meaningful, mission-driven work.
4. Gain stable compensation and long-term career growth.
5. Work in teams with good mentorship and engineering standards.

* What is your motivation scale?

I used a priority scale tied to role fit: high for ML-focused roles, mid for data analyst-oriented roles, and low for frontend-heavy roles. In this list that maps to 8 (high), 5 (mid), and 2 (low), with Infinity Labs set to 10 as my top priority as that is a career in which I have connections and a job offer.

**Chapter 4: Posting**
* How much time should this take?

About 15 minutes.

* What is your threshold for the number of posting a company might have?

Any active posting relevant to my path is enough to keep a company on the list; more postings increase urgency and priority.

* What is your scale for this column?

I used a 1-3 scale: 3 = many or clearly active openings, 2 = some active openings, 1 = few or limited openings.

**Summary**
A short reflection (250-300 words) on how prioritization can improve your job search.
Prioritization improves my job search because it helps me spend energy where I have the best combination of fit, interest, and opportunity. Before using the LAMP method, it was easy to apply broadly without much strategy, which made the process feel random and discouraging. With LAMP, I can rank companies by advocacy, motivation, and postings, then focus first on the entries that give me the strongest chance of progress.

My motivation scores make my goals clear: I want machine learning and AI-oriented work most, data-focused work second, and frontend-heavy roles lower. That keeps my applications aligned with the path I actually want to build long term. Setting Infinity Labs as my top priority reflects both high motivation and the fact that I already have real connections there. At the same time, including a broad list of 40 companies keeps me from relying on only one outcome.

The posting column also changes how I act week to week. Companies with higher posting counts get faster follow-up because timing matters, while lower-posting organizations stay on my list but do not consume as much time. My qualify column adds realism. I marked many companies as not currently qualified, which helps me avoid wasted effort and identify skill gaps I need to close.

Overall, prioritization gives me structure, reduces stress, and makes my search measurable. Instead of asking, "Where should I apply next?" I can follow a ranked plan, track progress, and adjust intentionally as new roles open and my qualifications improve.
