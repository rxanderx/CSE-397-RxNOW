**Chapter 5: Naturalize**
* How long should this task take?

About 20 minutes.

* What does Naturalize mean?

Naturalize means finding a way to create a real contact at a company where I currently have no connection. In other words, it is moving from "cold" to at least "some connection" so outreach is more effective.

* How many promising contacts should I identify? Why?

I should identify around 2-5 solid contacts per target company. I do not want to spread effort too thin, but I also do not want all my chances tied to one person who may never reply.

* What is your target Rate of Return?

A realistic target for responses is around 20-40%. Not everyone will respond, so the goal is consistency and quality outreach rather than expecting every message to work.


**Chapter 6: Advocacy**
* How long should this task take?

About 20 minutes.

* What is the 6-point email?

The 6-point email is a short outreach format designed to get a response without sounding like a job request. My understanding of the framework is:

1. Keep it short (roughly under 75-100 words).
2. Lead with a real connection or context.
3. Ask for advice/insight, not for a job.
4. State interest clearly and simply.
5. Use a direct question/request for a short conversation.
6. Keep the focus on learning from the contact.

* What are three troubleshooting solutions?

1. If no response, follow up after 3 business days with a short, polite message.
2. If still no response, follow up again around 7 business days or move to another contact.
3. If results stay weak, simplify the message and make it more specific to that person/company.

**Chapter 7: Track**
* How long should this task take?

About 10 minutes.

* What is the 3B7 Routine?

It is a follow-up rhythm: send initial outreach, wait 3 business days, send follow-up if needed, then check again at 7 business days. The point is to stay organized, avoid guessing, and keep momentum without being pushy.

**Summary**
A short reflection (250-300 words) on how prioritization can improve your job search.

Prioritization improves my job search because it turns a stressful, random process into something I can actually manage. If I try to chase every possible opportunity, I burn time and energy too quickly and end up with lower-quality outreach. Focusing first on companies and roles I actually care about gives each contact attempt more purpose.

Part 2 made it clear that the biggest difference is not just applying more, but applying smarter. Naturalizing contacts helps me move away from pure cold applications, which often feel like sending resumes into a black hole. The advocacy approach also makes outreach feel more professional and less awkward because the message is short, respectful, and focused on learning.

The tracking part is probably the most practical for me. I tend to either overthink follow-ups or forget them entirely. Using a 3B7 routine gives me structure and keeps me from making emotional decisions based on one non-response. It also helps me compare results across different companies and messages so I can improve over time.

I still do not think networking will ever feel completely natural to me, but this process gives me a repeatable system I can trust. Instead of hoping for one lucky break, I can run a consistent process: choose targets, send thoughtful outreach, track responses, and adjust. That makes the search feel less random and more like a plan I can execute.
