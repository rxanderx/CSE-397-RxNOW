## Part 2: Reflection & Learning Journal

Answer the following for each job you applied to. Number your responses accordingly.
* What did you already know about this type of job or company before applying?
* What did you learn that was new or surprising when reading the job description?
* Were there qualifications or responsibilities that you didn’t expect?
* How did you tailor your resume or cover letter to fit this job?
* How did this application process affect your confidence or understanding of the job market?

### 1. Infinity Labs - Machine Learning Engineer
Before applying, I already knew a little about Infinity Labs because my dad worked for the DoD and had some connections there, which helped me get an informational interview earlier. I already knew what the DoD was, roughly how some of that environment worked, and that this was the kind of direction I wanted more than anything else.

I was comfortable applying for this role because all of these are machine learning jobs, and I have spent a lot of personal time on ML and RL projects, including an RTS reinforcement learning project and some work with chess. One thing that surprised me was seeing tools I had not used before and needing to research them. In this case, Gymnasium for RL training stood out, so before the second interview I made sure I had a project using it that I could show off.

One qualification I did not expect was how much leadership and ownership showed up even in what looked like an entry-level role. I tailored my resume and project descriptions closely to the posting and highlighted the projects that best matched what they wanted. This whole process helped my confidence a lot, and it ended with a job offer.

### 2. NVIDIA - Machine Learning Intern
I already knew NVIDIA was one of the major names in machine learning because of its connection to GPUs and the broader AI ecosystem. What this role taught me is how closely machine learning work can be tied to high-performance systems and the tools that make modern AI possible.

I did not tailor my materials for this one. It was part of the broader effort to get hired, but it was still useful because it showed me how much strong ML work depends on understanding the compute side too.

### 3. TikTok - Machine Learning Engineer (University Graduate 2026)
I knew TikTok would expect strong machine learning fundamentals and likely care about large-scale recommendation systems. I also thought it was funny to really stop and consider how much machine learning goes into their suggestions and how much effort they would put into that. What this role taught me is how much graduate-level ML roles value ranking, product-scale experimentation, and strong software implementation.

I did not tailor my resume for this one, but it helped me see what top-tier consumer ML jobs expect from new grads.

### 4. Adobe - Machine Learning Engineer (2026 Graduate)
I expected Adobe to care about applied machine learning in a polished product environment. What this role taught me is that ML can be closely tied to user-facing creative tools and that product quality matters just as much as model quality.

I did not tailor my resume for this one. It was useful for comparing product-focused ML work against defense and systems-focused work.

### 5. Qualcomm - Machine Learning Engineer (College Grad)
I already expected Qualcomm to lean more heavily into optimized systems and technical depth. What this role taught me is that machine learning engineers also need to think about performance, hardware, and deployment constraints.

I did not tailor my materials for this job, but it gave me a better picture of how ML changes when it is closer to hardware.

### 6. XIFIN - Associate Machine Learning Engineer
I expected XIFIN to be a more practical associate-level ML role, and that made it useful as a comparison point. What this one taught me is that not every ML job is aiming at research scale or big-tech scale, and that there are strong opportunities to apply ML in business and healthcare-oriented settings too.

I did not tailor my resume for this one. It helped me understand the range of ML roles available and where an associate-level position might fit into a broader career path.
