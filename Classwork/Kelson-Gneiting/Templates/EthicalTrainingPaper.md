## Video Introduction
* Henry is a retired professional engineer and automobile executive.  He is still well-connected and is a lobbyist for a company called GUIDEME.  He is involved in an academia-industry-DOT (Department of Transportation) smart highway design project calls SANSHANDS.  The project goal is to develop specifications for automated highways and car control systems so that people won’t have to drive anymore.
* Laura, Henry’s older daughter, works at the DOT.  She is a professional engineer and will be the technical project manager on the SANSHANDS project.  As project manager, Laura will be responsible for compiling and recommending specifications for the computer control system that will guide R&D (research & development) and, ultimately, define the next generation of smart highways.  Her recommendations will be considered by the DOT Commissioner before being adopted.
* Julie is Henry’s younger daughter.  With her father’s finagling, she got her first engineering job with OUTOCAR, a local start-up company working with the university.  A major existing company, GUIDEME, is competing with OUTOCAR to take the SANSHANDS concept to the next level.

## Ethic Issues Found in the Movie
Types of ethical issues encountered by the characters include professional relationships, conflicts of interest, favoritism, confidentiality of proprietary information, intellectual property, sexual harassment, and individual privacy.  The characters disagree over tradeoffs between technical performance, safety, reliability, sustainability, flexibility, and cost.  They also find that political and social factors can influence technical decisions.

You will find many ethical issues in the movie, but here are a few examples of specific things you should look for to get you started:
* To what extent should engineers consider political factors and social impacts in their engineering work?
* If one technology is “best”, why should cost and other factors matter?
* Was it a conflict of interest for Henry’s daughter to be the project manager?  Why or why not?
* Are sexist comments disguised as “jokes” acceptable?
* What is proprietary information?  How should it be protected?
* Does tracking vehicle location cause a violation of privacy?
