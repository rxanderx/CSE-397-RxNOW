## Part 2: Reflection & Learning Journal

Answer the following for each job you applied to. Number your responses accordingly.
* What did you already know about this type of job or company before applying?
* What did you learn that was new or surprising when reading the job description?
* Were there qualifications or responsibilities that you didn’t expect?
* How did you tailor your resume or cover letter to fit this job?
* How did this application process affect your confidence or understanding of the job market?


## Part 3: Takeaways
Answer the following questions
* Which job was the best fit for you and why?
* What new skill, term, or expectation did you learn this week?
* What’s one thing you plan to improve or do differently next week in your job search?
 
## Part 4: Project-Based Growth Goal
Choose one new insight or skill gap you discovered from a job application and turn it into a mini-goal or project. This can be a technical skill, software, certification, soft skill, or concept you were unfamiliar with.

Answer the following:
* What did you learn that you didn’t know before this week’s applications?
    > (Example: “I didn’t realize that many jobs require familiarity with CRM software like Salesforce.”)
* Why is this important in your target industry or role?
    > (Example: “It’s used in sales and marketing roles to manage customer relationships and data.”)
* What specific mini-project or action will you take this week to start learning or building that skill?
    > (Example: “Watch a beginner tutorial on Salesforce and create a practice contact database.”)
* How will you track your progress?
    > (Example: “Upload a screenshot of my Salesforce dashboard and write 2–3 sentences about what I learned.”)
