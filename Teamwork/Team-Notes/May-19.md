---

### AI-Assisted Development Workflow
- Josh demonstrated how he has been using AI project workspaces (specifically Claude projects) to assist development.
- He explained the advantages of:
  - uploading SRS and technical documentation into a persistent AI project workspace,
  - maintaining project-wide context across chats,
  - and avoiding repeated copy-pasting of code into isolated AI conversations.
- Josh described how:
  - the AI can reference dozens of uploaded files,
  - maintain shared context across separate debugging or design chats,
  - and provide architecture-aware responses tied directly to project documents.
- Example use cases discussed:
  - Linux compatibility analysis,
  - debugging standard error output,
  - architecture review,
  - and technical improvement suggestions.
- The team responded positively to the workflow demonstration.

## Team Coordination Decisions

### Meeting Structure
- The team decided that additional formal meetings during the week may not be necessary unless blockers arise.
- Instead, the group agreed to:
  - communicate asynchronously through chat,
  - request ad-hoc one-on-one help sessions as needed,
  - and coordinate directly for backend or compatibility issues.

### Peer Support Workflow
- Josh encouraged team members to directly schedule short collaboration sessions with one another when specific technical help is needed.
- Backend troubleshooting and compatibility discussions were specifically mentioned as areas where smaller focused discussions may be more effective than full-team meetings.
